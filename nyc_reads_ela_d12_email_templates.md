# District 12 Email Guidance

## What
âž” Use **aligned templates** for *Before and After each* **Meeting**

## When
âž” Unless otherwise stated emails are to be sent within 24 hours of each meeting
â—† Hours to send emails: **8:00 - 5:00 PM** (*During Schools Days ONLY*)
  - If outside of the named hours Schedule Send for **8:00 AM the next School Day**
  - Do not send emails during school closure (**schedule send for the first date of return**)
    - *Ex: Schedule Send all Emails for Thursday September 25th @ 8:00 AM*

## Teacher Emails

While there is not a provided template for teachers emails, all coaches are to send a follow-up coaching email to teachers/teams after each visit.

â—† The teacher emails follow the same timing guidelines (**send during school hours, on school day**)

â—† Teacher emails are to be sent to teacher teams/groups/individuals and the school administration (**principal or assistant principal or capacity builder are to be CC'd on the email**)

â—† Teacher emails are to include a quick summary of the coaching day
  - Highlights from the coaching day (in classroom support or meetings or both)
  - One area of opportunity and one aligned next step (**this needs be shared and reviewed with teachers prior to sending in an email to clarify and provide support prior to implementation**)
  - Naming the next coaching day and any materials that teachers will need in preparation for it

## Tips/Tricks

1. Ensure that you change anything **highlighted** to reflect your schools information
2. Double check the school's information (*Principal Name, School, Grades Teams, etc..*)
3. Double check for all grammatical errors (*misspelled words, periods, etcâ€¦*)
4. Ensure all attachments and links are shared as **everyone can view**
5. Ensure all formats are aligned (**one font style is used for the entire email**)
   - *Ex: Georgia or Sans Serif*
6. **Use AI** to fill in the categories for the Coaching Summary Email (**plug in the necessary information- observation notes, details from the coaching day, and have AI plug the information into the template**)
   - Please note that **AI is a tool that operates using real information, real data, personal notes. It grows and adapts according to inputs however emails, feedback, notes have to match/align/be specific to your coaching at your designated school, sending emails with generic, overarching coaching reflections/feedback is *not permissible* and *not a best practice***
7. Create Email Groups on Gmail to ensure the right people are on the correct emails

**Summary and Highlights**

- Across this cycle, coaching support included lesson modeling, co-planning, student work analysis, and structured reflection. Teachers engaged in learning around gradual release, scaffolded and hearty questioning, and aligning small-group instruction to student data.
- **2nd Grade:** Teachers applied explicit instruction strategies, particularly during the "We Do" phase, and incorporated scaffolded questions to strengthen student talk and task clarity.
- **4th Grade:** Teachers improved lesson planning by embedding structured I Do, We Do, and You Do phases and practiced developing questions that support skill mastery.

**Teams Coached:(Teams/Type of Coaching)**

- **3rd Grade Team (Ms. H, Mr. K, Ms.R):** Group coaching via content meeting, collaborative internalization and planning. **In-the-Moment Coaching:** Provided during live instruction (*Mr. K and Ms.H*)

**Planning Highlight**

- Teachers used student work to surface misconceptions and adjust instruction in real time.
- Small-group planning became more targeted, with intentional grouping and skill alignment based on I-Ready and classroom data.
- Gradual release strategies became more consistent across both grades.

**Area(s) to Address**

- Teachers continue to build toward ensuring that independent tasks (You Do) fully align with the skill and offer the right level of support for students to demonstrate mastery without over-scaffolding.
- There is also a need for continued refinement of small-group lesson delivery, particularly in closing gaps through flexible, student-responsive planning.

**Bridge to Practice- Cycle Goal:** The Cycle 2 goal was to strengthen Tier 1 instruction through the use of explicit instruction techniques (*I Do, We Do, You Do*) and intentional lesson tailoring in both whole- and small-group settings. Teachers are continuing to internalize and apply these practices, setting a strong foundation for continued growth. **[Partially Met]**
